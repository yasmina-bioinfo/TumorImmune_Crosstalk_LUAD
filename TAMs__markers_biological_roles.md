
# TAM Subtype Markers and Biological Roles : Bloc 4A GSE243013 LUAD

## Overview
Eight TAM subclusters identified from cluster 7 (M2-like/immunosuppressive TAMs)
of the global TME annotation (Bloc 2). Resolution = 0.1, dims = 1:20, 20 PCs.
Total TAMs analyzed: 14,901 cells.
Cluster 6 excluded (lymphocyte contamination).

## 3. Literature References for Project Documentation

 **Cheng, S., Li, Z., et al.** "A pan-cancer single-cell landscape of myeloid cells." *Cell*, 2021. PMC8021415. 
    * *Project Relevance:* Establishes the foundational pan-cancer single-cell framework dividing pro-tumor myeloid subsets into metabolic/remodeling arms versus antigen presentation/chemokine arms.
 **Zhang, L., et al.** "SPP1+ Macrophages and the Orchestration of Spatially Organized Immunosuppression in Cancer." *Biomedicines*, 2026.
     *Project Relevance:* Explains how lipid-rich TAMs construct stromal barriers blocking physical CD8 T-cell entry, and details how IFN-$\gamma$ responsive subclusters anchor PD-L1 checkpoints.
 **Liu, Y., et al.** "SPP1+ macrophages in tumor immunosuppression: mechanisms and therapeutic implications." *Frontiers in Immunology*, 2025.
     *Project Relevance:* Synthesizes state-of-the-art evidence regarding how metabolic reprogramming of macrophages directly induces exhaustion and nutrient deprivation in CD8 effector populations.
**Lin Y, Chen Z, Zhao S, et al.** "Targeting SPP1+ TAMs associated with liver metastasis reverses immunosuppression and synergizes with immunotherapy in colorectal cancer." J Immunother Cancer. 2026;14(3):e014128. PMC13034337.
     *Project Relevance:* Demonstrates that SPP1+ TAMs construct an immunosuppressive niche blocking CD8 T cell infiltration and function. Targeting SPP1+ TAMs reverses exhaustion and synergizes with anti-PD-1. Although in colorectal cancer context, the mechanisms of SPP1+ TAM-mediated CD8 suppression are directly relevant to the immunosuppressive TAM clusters identified in our GSE243013 LUAD analysis.
**Göttlich C, et al.** "Immune Regulatory Processes of the Tumor Microenvironment under Malignant Conditions." Int J Mol Sci. 2021;22(24):13310. PMC8706102.
     *Project Relevance:* Comprehensive review of TME immunosuppressive mechanisms including Tregs, MDSCs, inhibitory receptors and cytokines. Provides biological context for the immunosuppressive TAM clusters identified in GSE243013 and supports the H2 hypothesis on extrinsic TME-mediated resistance to anti-PD-1.
